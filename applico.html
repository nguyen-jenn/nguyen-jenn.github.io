Overview

Whether it’s for a handful of job applications, or hundreds, it can be difficult to stay organized with the statuses as well as the various assignments necessary to apply. Applico is a web app designed for anybody who is job searching and looking to stay organized and on top of their deadlines.

This was a personal project and I was the sole designer working on this project during Fall 2022.

Problem

As a recent post-grad, many of my friends are currently job searching, and this sparked my initial interest in learning more about how to make the process easier for them, given the already stressful situation of trying to get a job within this current job market.

However, post-grads aren’t the only ones looking for a job, I wanted to ensure this web app is universal in helping anybody who is job searching.

Goals

1. Create a convenient platform that provides organization for users that are job searching
2. Encourage users to stay on top of their tasks and deadlines

Research

First, I interviewed 7 people who have job searched before, or are currently job searching.

Insights

1. Job tracking should feel personalized.

People enjoyed using Google docs since they are able to color code and set up categories as they need for the type of career they are pursuing.

2. Users would like to have organization but don’t know where to start or note that it is time-consuming to start it.

One user commented that they used Microsoft Word because that was what they knew how to use at the time, however noted that they did not have the best experience.

Another user commented they enjoy using Google Docs, however the downside is that it takes a while to set up.

3. Managing tasks and their deadlines should be easy and help the user not procrastinate.

Users commented how it’s easy to get distracted or become unmotivated to complete tasks they needed for job hunting.

Turning Insights into Design Ideas


- Statuses are pre-colored by default.

- Categories such as “Job Title, Status” are pre-made, however users are able to delete or create their own categories based on what they need.

- Users can create “tasks” that they need to complete, and there will be a checklist system that lets users know what they need to work on.

- Users can stay up to date with deadlines and upcoming important dates using a calendar system that syncs with all other features in the app.

Competitive Analysis

Other than Google Sheets, I wanted to see if there were any web apps already available that would achieve the same function of job tracking. I also included LinkedIn because one user that I interviewed mentioned that they primarily job searched on LinkedIn and that they were able to track their applications on there since LinkedIn would compile which job postings they already applied to.

A/B Testing

As I began to ideate what I wanted the look of the web app to be like, I researched existing designs of various web platforms. The two that I selected as potential designs were the dashboard look versus a navigation bar look.

I mocked up these two designs and created a poll for people to select which design they liked more.

A
B

21/31 of the people who participated in the poll voted that they liked design A more than design B.

*With hindsight, next time I would design the A/B testing differently as there were two people who questioned if design A would have a collapsible column, which would have affected their answer.

Even though they were two similar yet distinct designs, it seemed design A had the potential to function differently than design B, which was something I was not aware at first prior to creating the poll. If I were to re-do this A/B testing, I would ensure that I will only test a single variable so that the result was accurate.

Reflection

I am happy with how this project turned out! Since my first large project (inspop) was a mobile app, I wanted to challenge myself with a new platform, this time, a web app. My process in designing this was different from inspop, since for this, I had much more space and needed to ensure I did not create too much negative space.

If I were to continue this project, I would design what it would look like to change a status of a job application. One idea I have for the column view is being able to click and drag boxes across columns, instantly updating the status in the backend. Another idea I have is for list view, for which users can click on the job’s status and a drop-down menu of other statuses would appear, allowing the user to easily switch between statuses.
