The Beginning

Many people tend to doodle on their assignments or tests in school, but for me, I did faux lettering back in high school instead. Faux lettering is a technique how to imitate lettering with simply a pen or pencil, a great way to introduce beginners into lettering without spending money on expensive brush pens. That is how my journey into handlettering got started.

For my Secret Santa exchange in the winter of 2018, I asked for packs of dual brush pens on my wishlist, hoping someone would buy them for me. And my Secret Santa did!

I remember struggling with making my lines consistent at the beginning since I wasn't used to the feeling of manipulating the brush pen in order to get a broader down stroke or a crisp up stroke. The rest of 2018 and 2019, I spent practicing lettering during my free time as I balanced college classes and extracurriculars.

Opening up an Etsy

During college, a group of friends and I co-founded a club called Made with Love. The purpose of the club was to create handcrafted items to donate to the local community! We also strived to introduce various arts and crafts to our club members.

During my two years on board, I was able to lead a beginner workshop TWICE to teach our club members the basics of handlettering! The first time around, I did not prepare anything and simply taught freehanded. The second time, I prepared lettering worksheets for club members to print out and practice as they followed along with my directions.

After those experiences, I realized that I enjoyed teaching others how to handletter and decided to open up an Etsy selling beginner worksheets! I ended up designing a 40+ page workbook and have made 7 sales so far!
